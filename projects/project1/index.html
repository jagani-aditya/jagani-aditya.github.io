Humanoid Robot

The Humanoid Robot is a 3D printed life-size robot whose design is similar to the Inmoov. The primary aim of developing the humanoid robot was to impart the physical as well as the mental traits of a human being. For instance, we imbibed perception algorithms that can not only recognize humans, maintain eye contact with them, but also respond well while conversing with a human. We are currently developing gaits for dynamic stability by imparting robust controllers using crucial mathematical concepts such as Kinematics, Dynamics, Controls, etc.

The Robot head consists of a gimbal designed to control the eye movement using servo motors. The software enables the robot to track people in the frame by actuating its eyes. We have further extended the algorithm to control the servo motors located in the neck for horizontal, vertical and tilting movement. The motors located in the eyes and the neck work in unison to track people and maintain eye contact with them.

Our Makers are delegated tasks according to their experience and interests. A few belong to the mechanical department, who design intricate parts for the robot, while the electroncis department takes care of programming the robot to impart characteristics that closely resemble to humans using robust, concise and reliable algorithms


We use FlashForce 3D Printers to print strengthened, affordable and long-lasting 3D parts using ABS or PLA materials. Fingers and Legs are being printed in the GIF below. Sometimes, we run into problems such as obtaining unfinished jobs, however, We always find a way around it!!

We improvise our designs constantly with primary aim of making the robot as dynamic, quick and stable as possible. We have also integrated a PID system for Stance Hold using a Potentiometer for joint angle feedback.


The Maker Space also consists of a Laser/ Engraving Machine, which we extensively use to cut MDF and Acralyic sheets to model a stand for the bipeds on which we test the behavior of our controllers for its stability and performance
